About Marissa

Welcome to my portfolio site!

I'm a growing web developer, self-taught using The Odin Project, FreeCodeCamp.org, Wes Bos's JS coursework, and other various resources. Proficient in HTML5, CSS3, Git, GitHub, and Bash shell command basics, I am now focusing on honing my JavaScript skills and feeding my ever-evolving passion for the language.

When first starting out with JavaScript, I began with Vanilla JS--completely framework free. This has allowed me to get a thorough grasp on the inner workings and fundamentals of JavaScript before applying frameworks--my next step. I am currently learning React.js but am curious about other frameworks as well.

As I continue my rapid growth, I remain open to opportunities to continue learning alongside other like-minded individuals. If interested in working together, please visit my Connect page for contact details, or check out my blog for more about me!

Thanks for visiting my site!

More About Me...

In May of 2017, I earned my bachelor's degree in English from SUNY Geneseo, a small, state liberal arts college in Upstate New York. During my time there, I explored a broad spectrum of passions from sports, to cultural studies, to writing and academics.

While in undergrad, I joined The Canonicity Project, a student-led research group started one year prior by my close friends and mentors, Sean Fischer and Ben Wach, as well as our research advisor Dr. Gillian Paku. I contributed as a research assistant, helping to organize, format, and enter data for analysis. This was my earliest exposure to data analysis programs (R, specifically) and app creation--serving as the kernal around which I would eventually build my love of coding and web app development.

My biggest non-academic passion while in college was competing for Geneseo's cross country and track teams. Some of my personal-bests included a 5:08 mile, 4:47 in the 1500, 10:20 in the 3k, and 18:15 in the 5k. In 2014, I ran at my first NCAA national championship meet, contributing to a 5th place team finish and placing in the top 50 individually. Later, in 2016 and 2017, I had the chance to serve as a team captain, contributing to a 3rd place team finish and placing in the top 100 individually. During my time as a Geneseo athlete, I earned USTFCCCA All-Academic honors, was named an All-Region athlete and All-Conference Hall of Fame Inductee, and also served on the Student-Athlete Advisory Committee.
